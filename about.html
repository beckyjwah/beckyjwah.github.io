My Life

Ever since I was little, I drew a lot and did arts and crafts. Starting in the fourth grade, art class became separate from homeroom classes. Art was the only way where I can relax and express how I feel. I had learning difficulties when I was younger, but I felt like I have a connection with art. I took art seriously. I see what my teacher draws and I understand when my teacher teaches us his techniques. Not that I was not serious in my other classes, but it was hard in the other classes. My mom quit her job to help me with my learning difficulties. I felt that I did not belong anywhere; I felt that I was really behind and was the always the last one. I had no idea what my teacher would be talking about in class. I felt like a cloud just drifting and no matter how much I tried to pay attention I could not seem to understand at all on what was going on, but what ever she wrote down on the board, I wrote down in my notebook, but art was different. My mom always supported what I want to do. So, in the summer of 2015, I took two classes at SAIC. In the morning I took figure drawing and in the afternoon, I took water base paints for two weeks. It was a new experience and I got lots of critiques. I find critiques very important because they make me think and improve my art. In 2016, I took a class at SAIC and at Illinois Institute of Technology on architecture. It was a totally different from drawing and painting. Architecture is a completely new language in arts.
